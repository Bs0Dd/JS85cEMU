Virtual encrypter


    The appearance of the encrypter is similar to the real one, with the exception of the "initial settings" button, which is located on the back side of the device, therefore, for convenience, it is placed on the front side in the emulator.

Options panel


    The options panel consists of 4 sections and is intended for configuring the emulator. Here you can pause or restart the emulator, enable or disable vibration when pressing keys (mobile devices only). It is possible to load RAM or ROM from files and save the contents of RAM. Here you can adjust the operating speed for standard and turbo modes and switch between them. Some debugging and experimental functions are also available, and you can also open a window with a layout diagram, a help window, the "About" page, and show/hide the Debugger or the Xchanger utility.

Xchanger utility


    The utility is designed to simplify interaction with a virtual encrypter. Its main purpose is automated input (via keystroke emulation or directly into RAM) and extraction of information from the encrypter. It also has a system for generating new long-term keys (DKL / LTK) and the ability to extract a previously recorded DKL from a virtual device.

Debugger


    A debugger is a debugging complex, rather useful for those who want to research the firmware. Displays the state of registers (including special ones) and allows you to change their values. You can control the operation of the device (the "pause" and "restart" keys are duplicated from the options panel), debug the firmware in step-by-step mode, and set a breakpoint. There is a RAM editor, with the ability to view content in "bytes" and "words" (order from low to high) modes. The complex also includes a lite-disassembler for viewing currently performed operations. If desired, any command can be replaced using lite-assembler. You can switch display and input modes between hexadecimal and octal.
